Game Length

  • Players can play for any agreed on amount of time
  • The standard unit of play time is a Han-Chan session
  • A Han-Chan Session is:
    • Every player gets to be dealer twice
    • Meaning playing through two rounds
  • Half, One and Two Han-chans are popular game lengths

Ready Hand

  • A Ready Hand or Tenpai, is when a player is waiting on the last tile to complete their hand
  • This hand would be in Tenpai, waiting on the White Dragon.
  • This hand needs to at least two tiles to declare a win, so it is not in Tenpai

Calling

  • Calling allows you to add a dealt tile to your hand
  • To Call:
    • You say which call you are going to make
    • Reveal the tiles that you will use to finish the meld
    • Take the discarded tile and add it to the reveled tiles
    • These tiles stay on the table face up
    • The taken tile is turned horizontal and added to the meld to show which player you took the tile from
      • This horizontal rule is so that Furiten can be enforced
    • Discard for your turn and proceed with the game in the normal turn order
  • Calling opens your hand, which limits how you can score points
  • For detailed information on what hands need to be closed, check the Han/Yaku page
  • There are three different kinds of calls:
    • Chi - A Run
      • Chi is called when a discarded tile would complete a run meld
      • Chi can only be called when the player before you discards

      • Pon is called when a discarded tile would complete a set meld

    • Kan - Four of a Kind
      • Kan only counts as a meld, so to get the required hand, you need to draw an additional tile
      • You draw this tile from the dead wall
      • Kan also reveals another dora
      • Two Kinds of Kan
        • Open Kan - Four of a Kind
          • An Open Kan can be made two ways
            • Having three of a kind and seeing the fourth tile be dealt
            • Having a Pon of a tile and then drawing the fourth tile from the wall
          • Declaring an open Kan, reveal a new dora after you discard
          • A Kan Made from a player's discard on the right
        • Closed Kan - Four of a Kind
          • A Closed Kan does not open your hand
          • Closed Kan can only be made from having all four tiles within your hand
          • You do not have to immediately call a Kan if you have all four tiles
          • You can declare a Kan even if you did not draw one of the four this turn
          • When declaring an open Kan, reveal a new Dora after you draw, but before you discard

Furiten

  • Furiten is attempting to call a tile that you have already discarded
  • If you have the 5 Man in your discard, you cannot call ron on any 5 Man for the rest of the round
  • Furiten does not affect Chi, Pon or Kan
  • Furiten applies to all possible waits
