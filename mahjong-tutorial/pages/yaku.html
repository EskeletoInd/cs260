One Han Closed Only

Menzenchin tsumohou
Closed Only		 This yaku may apply to any closed hand. Any player who draws a winning tile with a closed hand is awarded this yaku.
Riichi
Closed Only		 This is a special yaku applied to closed tenpai hands, which requires the player to make a declaration and "wager".
Ippatsu
Closed Only		 This is applied when the player calling riichi wins within 4 tile draws/discards. In addition, the cycle must not be interrupted by tile calls.
All Sequences
Closed Only		 Or Pinfu, this is a hand that does not gain fu based on composition, other than that of a closed ron.
Pure Double Chi
Closed Only		 Or Iipikou, This hand includes two identical sequences.

One Han

Last Draw
May be open		 The hand is completed with the last tile on the wall.
Last Discard
May be open		 The hand is completed with the last discarded tile.
Kaihou
May be open		 Awarded to a player upon winning with a tile from the dead wall.
Robbing a Kan
May be open		 or Chankan, The winning tile for a hand is called on another player's Kan. Essentially, the tile needed to complete a Kan is stolen to complete a winning hand.
All Simples
May be open		 A hand without terminals or honor tiles.
Round Wind Triplet
May be open		 A hand with a meld of the round wind.
Seat Wind Triplet
May be open		 A hand with a meld of a players wind.
Dragon Triplet
May be open		 A hand with a meld of dragons.

Two Han

Double Riichi
Closed Only		 Riichi is declared with a dealt hand before the first discard.
Outside Hand
May be open; Loses 1 han		 All tile groups contain at least 1 terminal or honor.
Triple Chi - Sanshoku doujun
May be open; Loses 1 han		 Three sequences have the same number across the three different suits.
Pure Straight
May be open; Loses 1 han		 Or Ittsu, This hand has a complete sequence of 1 through 9 of a single suit. As a note, the sequence of 1-9 are actually three groups of sequences numbered 123, 456, and 789.
All Pon - Toi Toi
May be open		 The entire hand is composed of triplets.
Three Concealed Pon - Shanankou
May be open		 The hand includes three groups of triplets (or closed quads) that have been formed without calling any tiles. The fourth group can be an open triplet or sequence.
Triple Pon - Sanshoku doukou
May be open		 The hand includes three groups of triplets with the same number.
Three Concealed Pon - Sanankou
May be open		 The hand includes three groups of triplets (or closed quads) that have been formed without calling any tiles. The fourth group can be an open triplet or sequence.
Triple Kan - Sankantsu
May be open		 This hand includes three Kans.
Seven Pairs - Chiitoitsu
Closed only		 This hand is composed of seven pairs. It is one of two exceptions to the standard 4 melds and a pair pattern.
All Terminals and Honors - Honroutou
May be open		 The hand is composed of nothing but terminals and honors.
Little Three Dragons - Shousangen
May be open		 The hand contains two sets of 3 dragon tiles and a pair of the third dragon tiles.

Three Han

Half Flush - Honitsu
May be open; Loses 1 Han		 This is a single suit hand mixed with some honor tiles.
Terminals In All Groups - Junchan Taiyao
May be open; Loses 1 Han		 All melds contain at least one terminal.
Twice Pure Double Chi - Ryanpeikou
Closed Only		 This hand has two sets of "iipeikou". This hand does not combine with chii toitsu, even though the hand can be interpreted as one.

Six Han

Full Flush - Chinitsu
May be open; Loses 1 Han		 This hand is composed entirely of tiles from only one of the three suits.

Yakuman

Counted Yakuman - Kazoe yakuman
May be open		 This is a special class of yakuman, where a hand is composed of 13+ han based on the combination of above regular yaku and/or dora.
Thirteen Orphans - Kokushi musou
Closed Only		 This hand has one of each of the 13 different terminal and honor tiles plus one extra terminal or honour tile. Some rules may allow double yakuman for a 13-wait set.
Four Concealed Pon - Suuankou
Closed only		 This hand is composed of four groups of closed triplets.
Big Three Dragons - Daisangen
May be open		 This hand possesses three melds of all the dragons.
Little Four Winds - Shousuushii
May be open		 This hand has three groups (triplets or quads) of the wind tiles plus a pair of the fourth kind.
Big Four Winds - Daisuushii
May be open		 This hand has four groups (triplets or quads) of all four wind tiles.
All Honors - Tsuuiisou
May be open		 This hand is composed entirely of tiles from only one of the three suits.
All Terminals - Chinroutou
May be open		 Every group of tiles are composed of terminal tiles.
All Green - Ryuuiisou
May be open		 A hand composed entirely of green tiles: 2, 3, 4, 6 and 8 Sou and/or Hatsu.
or
Nine Gates - Chuuren poutou
Closed only		 A hand consisting of the tiles 1112345678999 in the same suit plus any one extra tile of the same suit.
Any Pin Tile
Four Kan - Suukanstu
May be open		 Any hand with four calls of kan.

Initial Yakuman

Blessing of Heaven - Tenhou
Closed only		 The dealer hand is a winning hand even before discarding a tile.
Blessing of Earth - Chiihou
Closed only		 The non-dealer hand is a winning hand with the first tile draw.

Special Case

This yaku does not deal with a players hand, but rather the players discards. By default, this hand is set to mangan.
Honorable Discards - Nagashi mangan
closed only		 All the discards are terminals and/or honors. In addition, none of these discards were called by other players.
