Basic Rules of Mahjong

Tiles and Suits

  • 3 Numbered Suits (1-9)
    • The Dot or Pin Tiles
    • The Bamboo or Sou Tiles
    • The Character or Man Tiles
  • The Honor Tiles
    • East, South, West and North Wind Tiles
    • White, Green and Red Dragon Tiles
  • Four copies of each tile
  • Total of 136 tiles

Setup

  • Each player starts with 25,000 points
  • Each player creates a wall of 17x2 tiles
  • These walls become the draw pile
  • The dealer will then roll the dice to decide where to split the wall
  • The 7x2 Tiles to the left of the split becomes the dead wall
  • After the wall break, players take turns drawing tiles until they have 13 tiles
  • These are the players starting hands

Seating Positions

  • The Dealer is always seated as the east wind
  • Winds are then assigned counter clockwise in the pattern East, South, West, North
  • The turn order moves counter clockwise, with the dealer getting the first draw and discard

Objective

  • The Objective of the game is to complete your hand before anyone else
  • While players avoid playing into each other's hands
  • A complete hand is composed of three melds and a pair.
  • A meld either of the following:
    • A Set
    • A Run
  • A legal hand must have one or more Han to be worth any points
  • You can see legal Han on the Han/Yakupage
  • Two Different ways to declare a win:
    • Tsumo: Winning off a teal that you drew from the Wall
    • Ron: Winning off an opponents discard

Round Maintence

  • After each round points are distrubed to the player who completed their hand
  • Different hands are worth different amounts of points
  • Points are exchanged and tiles shuffled
